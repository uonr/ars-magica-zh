### Expenditure

The successful running of a covenant requires a multitude of different supplies and goods, which, if the covenant does not produce them itself, need to be purchased. At the most basic level, there is the need to feed, clothe, and shelter the covenfolk, and to provide laboratory equipment and writing materials for the magi. Beyond that, the grogs need weapons and armor, the defenses need to be maintained, luxury items might be purchased, construction may be undertaken, travel and hospitality need to be paid for, and so on. Even for a small spring covenant, these costs can easily amount to 50 pounds per year; larger covenants may spend five or even ten times that much.

*结社*的成功运营需要大量不同的物资与商品，凡*结社*自身无法生产者，均需对外采购。最基本的层面包括：为*结社民*提供饮食、衣物与住所，以及为*法师*提供*工坊*设备和书写材料。除此之外，*龙套*们需要武器与护甲，防御工事需要维护，奢侈品或许也要采购，建筑工程可能随时上马，旅行与接待也须花费，如此种种，不一而足。即便是一个小型的春季*结社*，这些费用每年轻易便能累积到 50 磅；规模较大的*结社*每年花费甚至可能达到五倍乃至十倍之多。

To start with, you should determine the normal yearly expenditure of the covenant — the costs of the things which are the same every year. First, you need to determine how many people of each type live at the covenant (see the Covenfolk Categories section below). Then, total up the number of points of inhabitants, weapons and armor, and laboratories, and determine the cost in each category of expenditure listed below, or refer to the Yearly Expenditure summary. This total usually remains static unless the point totals change (for example, the covenant employs more covenfolk, or the magi upgrade their labs).

首先，你需要确定*结社*每年的常规支出——即那些年年如此的固定花费。第一步，确定*结社*中每类人员各有多少（见下方「*结社民*类别」一节）。然后，汇总居民、武器与护甲以及*工坊*的积分总数，依照下列各支出类别确定相应费用，或参阅年度支出汇总表。除非积分总数发生变化（例如*结社*雇用了更多*结社民*，或*法师*升级了其*工坊*），否则这一总数通常保持不变。

Every year, there is also at least one pound's worth (usually considerably more) of sundry expenses — one-off items of expenditure, such as travel, or the purchase of books. More details of such exceptional expenses can be found in *Covenants* and *City and Guild*. These extra costs should be noted, totaled at the end of the year, and added to the normal yearly expenditure to give you that year's total expenditure. Subtract this from the income for the year, and add any surplus to (or if there is a shortfall, subtract it from) the covenant's stores.

每年还会有至少价值一磅（通常远不止于此）的各项杂费，即一次性支出，例如旅行费用或书籍采购。此类特殊开支的更多详情可参见 *Covenants* 与 *City and Guild*。这些额外费用应逐一记录，于年末汇总，再加上常规年度支出，即为当年的总支出。将此总支出从当年收入中扣除，若有盈余则加入*结社*储备，若有亏空则从中扣减。

#### Covenfolk Categories

For the purposes of accounting, the covenfolk (that is, everyone living at the covenant apart from magi and companions) should be separated into the following categories. (For characters that have a dual vocation, simply pick the most appropriate category.)

#### 结社民类别 (Covenfolk Categories)

就账务而言，*结社民*（即居住于*结社*中、除*法师*与*同伴*以外的所有人）应划分为以下类别。（对于身兼两职的角色，只需选择最为贴切的类别即可。）

**Craftsmen:** People with a Craft Ability, employed by the covenant to practice their craft and produce wares for the covenant, such as a smith, a carpenter, or a weaver. Craftsmen allow for cost savings in one or more categories of expenditure — the covenant does not need to spend so much on buying wares from outside. The exceptions are book-binders and illuminators, who do not save costs, but instead allow the covenant to produce quality books.

**工匠**：拥有工艺*技能*、受*结社*雇用以从事本业并为*结社*生产物品的人，例如铁匠、木匠或织工。工匠可在一个或多个支出类别中为*结社*节省开支——*结社*无需在对外采购上花费那么多。但书籍装订师与抄本绘图师是例外，他们不能节省开支，而是使*结社*得以制作优质书籍。

**Dependents:** Residents supported by the covenant who do no useful work, such as children, idle spouses, the infirm, and retirees. Children should only be counted from the age of five onwards (children younger than five can be assumed to consume a negligible amount of resources). Once they are old enough to work, children can be moved to another category.

**受养者**：由*结社*供养、不从事任何有用工作的居民，例如儿童、闲散配偶、病弱者与退休人员。儿童仅从五岁起计入（五岁以下的儿童可视为消耗资源微乎其微）。一旦年龄足以工作，儿童便可转入其他类别。

**Grogs:** Full-time soldiers whose primary vocation is the martial defense of the covenant and its magi.

***龙套***：全职士兵，其主要职责是对*结社*及其*法师*实施武装防卫。

**Laborers:** Workers who gather food for the covenant, such as farmhands, herders, fishermen, or hunters. Laborers allow for cost saving on Provisions, since the covenant does not need to buy so much food.

**劳工**：为*结社*采集食物的工人，例如农工、牧人、渔夫或猎手。劳工可在粮食供给方面为*结社*节省开支，因为*结社*无需购买那么多食物。

**Servants:** Essential folk who prepare and serve the food, clean, and perform other household tasks, such as maids, cooks, bakers, errand-boys, stablehands, and the like.

**仆役**：负责备餐送食、打扫清洁及其他家务的不可或缺的人员，例如女佣、厨师、面包师、跑腿小厮与马夫等。

**Specialists:** Individuals who occupy some important role in the hierarchy of running the covenant, such as an autocrat, a steward, a chamberlain, a turb captain, or a scribe. Hermetic apprentices also count as specialists.

**专员**：在*结社*管理体系中担任重要职位的人员，例如总管、管家、宫务官、护卫队长或书写员。赫尔墨斯*学徒*同样计入专员。

**Teamsters:** People who are employed to procure provisions and supplies from elsewhere and transport them to the covenant, typically one or more market buyers and a team of wagoners or hauliers. Some covenants are served by traveling merchants, in which case the teamsters do not belong to the covenant. However they still need to be paid and accounted for, since it is more expensive to buy wares on your doorstep than to travel to market and buy them there — either way, the covenant has to bear the haulage costs.

**运输工**：受雇从外地采办物资并运回*结社*的人员，通常包括一名或多名市场采购员以及一支运货车队或驮运队。部分*结社*由巡回商人提供服务，此时运输工并不隶属于*结社*；但仍需付款并计入账目，因为让商人送货上门比自行前往市场采购更为昂贵——无论哪种方式，*结社*都须承担运输费用。

You may employ as many or as few of each type of covenfolk as you like, except that there is a minimum required number of servants and teamsters. There is also a limit to the number of laborers and craftsmen that can be usefully employed (see the Cost Saving section below).

各类*结社民*的人数可多可少，悉听尊便，但仆役与运输工须满足最低人数要求。劳工与工匠的有效雇用人数也有上限（见下方「节省开支」一节）。

To determine the minimum number of servants and teamsters for your covenant, proceed as follows: Total up the points of inhabitants (see insert), including everyone except laborers, servants, and teamsters. For every 10 points of this total, you need 2 servants. Add the points for these servants to the total. Now, subtract twice the number of laborers. For every 10 points of this total, you need 1 teamster. Finally, recompute the total points of inhabitants, including all laborers, servants, and teamsters.

要确定*结社*所需的仆役与运输工最低人数，请按以下步骤操作：汇总居民积分（见插页），包含除劳工、仆役和运输工以外的所有人。每满 10 点积分，需配备 2 名仆役。将这些仆役的积分加入总数。再减去劳工人数的两倍。所得总数每满 10 点，需配备 1 名运输工。最后，将劳工、仆役与运输工全部纳入，重新计算居民积分总数。

Number of Servants required: 2 for every 10 points of (all inhabitants, excluding laborers, servants, and teamsters)

所需仆役人数：每满 10 点积分（所有居民，不含劳工、仆役与运输工）配备 2 名

Number of Teamsters required: 1 for every 10 points of (all inhabitants, excluding laborers and teamsters - 2 \* number of laborers)

所需运输工人数：每满 10 点积分（所有居民，不含劳工与运输工，减去劳工人数 × 2）配备 1 名

Take the total of the points for all the laboratories in the covenant. For an Upkeep score of between –5 (the lowest possible) and +5, the number of points per laboratory is as specified in the above table. For example, a standard laboratory (Upkeep 0) equates to 10 points. Beyond +5, the number of extra points gained per level of Upkeep increase is equal to the new Upkeep score times ten. For example, increasing the Upkeep from +5 to +6 increases the number of points by 60 (+6 multiplied by ten), taking it from 150 to 210. Thus, for a laboratory with an Upkeep of +2 or greater, the number of points is equal to ten times the number of experience points needed to buy an Art score with the same

value as the Upkeep, as specified in the Advancement Table, page 375. For example, a lab with an Upkeep of +15 equates to 1200 points, ten times the cost of an Art score of 15 (120 experience points). There is no upper limit on an Upkeep score, except the covenant's ability to pay for it!
