## 结社民的组织方式 (The Organization of Covenfolk)

*结社*中*龙套*的组织方式，可能在*结社*创建之初便已规划妥当，并随着规模扩大而有意调整；也可能是在*结社*历史中自然演化而成。本节介绍几种可供构建*结社*组织的概念。自然，各种方式可以混合搭配——例如，*结社*的战士可能按等级制组织，而仆役与专员则可能按族群划分。*龙套*的组织方式对*结社*的氛围与风格有重要影响，*剧团*应当花些心思考虑自己偏好哪种制度。这些问题以及更广义上的*结社民*生活，可参见 *Grogs* 第二章。

### Hierarchy

A hierarchical organization of a covenant's grogs is perhaps the most commonly found. It is the least work for the magi, as they have a small number of officials reporting to them, who then marshal their subordinates to complete the tasks at hand. The two most significant hierarchical models adopted are those of the noble household and the military.

### 等级制 (Hierarchy)

等级制是*结社*中*龙套*最常见的组织方式。对*法师*而言，这是最省力的模式：他们只需面对少数几位官员汇报，由这些官员再统领下属完成各项任务。最常采用的两种等级制模式，分别是贵族府邸模式与军事模式。

#### A Noble Household

Covenants who employ a large number of servants and specialists often adopt an organizational structure that mimics that of the noble household. This is a natural model to adopt, since examples of how well it functions can be found throughout Mythic Europe. However, this level of complexity is only really needed if the covenant is a large one, or is masquerading as a lord of the manor. At its most basic level this organization approximates the norm for covenants since the three chief officials in a noble household are the **steward**, the **chamberlain**, and the **marshal**.

#### 贵族府邸 (A Noble Household)

雇用了大量仆役与专员的*结社*，往往会采用模仿贵族府邸的组织结构。这是一种顺理成章的选择，因为整个*神话欧洲*都可以找到它运作良好的实例。不过，只有当*结社*规模较大，或需要伪装成庄园领主时，才真正需要这种复杂程度的组织。在最基本的层面上，这一架构与*结社*的惯常做法大体相同——贵族府邸的三位首席官员正是**管家**（steward）、**宫务官**（chamberlain）和**元帅**（marshal）。

The steward (or seneschal) is in control of the lord's lands and the keep, and is often a knight.

**管家**（或称总管）负责统管领主的土地与城堡，通常由骑士担任。

The steward sometimes has an under-steward called the steward of the household, or fills this role himself in smaller households. Any miscellaneous specialists, such as craftsmen employed directly by the household, fall under the steward's purview.

在规模较小的府邸中，管家有时会亲自兼任府邸内务管家一职；在较大的府邸中，也可能设有一名副管家专职负责。所有杂项专员，例如府邸直接雇用的工匠，均归管家管辖。

The chamberlain (or treasurer, or wardrober) is the chief clerical officer, and is occasionally a subordinate of the steward. The wardrobe is a special room where valuables are kept, and serves as the noble's treasury.

**宫务官**（或称财务官、衣物保管官）是首席文职官员，有时隶属于管家之下。"衣物室"（wardrobe）是存放贵重物品的专用房间，充当贵族的私人库房。

The marshal is the leader of the household knights or men-at-arms. He is also in charge of the horses and needs of the stables. In some households, he is considered subordinate to the steward, but in most covenants using this model, this is not true.

**元帅**（marshal）是府邸骑士或男兵的领袖，同时负责马匹与马厩事务。在某些府邸中，他被视为管家的下属，但在采用此模式的大多数*结社*中，情况并非如此。

More information on the disposition of a noble household is given in Lords of Men.

关于贵族府邸组织的更多信息，请参见 *Lords of Men*。

#### Military Hierarchy

Rather than dividing the covenfolk by role, a covenant can take a more military approach. In this organizational system, the covenfolk are divided into squads, each of which consists of soldiers and their auxiliary staff. An army in Mythic Europe is largely free of intermediate ranks. The marshal is in charge of his lord's household troops, including the knights and menat-arms. If his lord goes to war, the marshal takes charge of the men-at-arms, while the lord commands the knights; otherwise, he leads the knights himself (in lieu of the tenant-in-chief, thus the later rank "lieutenant") and appoints a captain to command the men-at-arms.

#### 军事等级制 (Military Hierarchy)

*结社*也可以采用更具军事色彩的方式，而非按职能划分*结社民*。在这一组织体系中，*结社民*被分成若干小队，每支小队由战士及其辅助人员组成。*神话欧洲*的军队在很大程度上不设中间层级：元帅统领领主的府邸军队，包括骑士与男兵。当领主出征时，元帅负责男兵，而领主亲统骑士；若领主未亲自出征，元帅则代为率领骑士（"lieutenant"一词即源于此，意为“代替上级者”），并另委任一名队长统领男兵。

This model is appropriate for military-minded covenants, but not necessarily only them. In a similar way to dividing covenfolk into nations (see later), this structure can fill a role where, for example, the covenant is divided over several geographically separate sites. The difference to the system of nations is that, in a military hierarchy, each squad is not independent of each other.

这一模式适合军事取向的*结社*，但未必仅限于此类*结社*。与将*结社民*划分为族群的方式（见后文）类似，这一结构在*结社*分布于多个地理上相互分隔的据点时，也能发挥重要作用。它与族群制度的区别在于，在军事等级制中，各小队之间并非相互独立。

*Example: Traditional covenants like to follow the early Roman model, where the manus ("hand," singular and plural), comprised of a squad of four men plus their leader (the quincurion), is the basic unit. A decuria is made of two manus, with the leader of one manus, the decurion, superior to the other. Up to 10 decuriae make up the centuria, commanded by a centurion.*

*示例：传统*结社*喜欢沿用古罗马早期的模式。其中，manus（"手"，单复数同形）由四人小队加上其领袖（五人长 quincurion）组成，是最基本的单位。一个 decuria 由两个 manus 组成，其中一个 manus 的领袖（十人长 decurion）高于另一个。多达十个 decuriae 构成一个 centuria（百人队），由百夫长（centurion）统领。*

### Nations

Another method of organizing grogs is by nation. A nation of grogs consists of servants, specialists, and soldiers — possibly even companions and magi. The identifying feature of a nation is that it cuts across roles. One's nation may be determined by a number of methods. Sometimes new members are assigned at random, either by lot or in order to fill gaps in the ranks. Otherwise, a recruit might join the nation of the person (magus or otherwise) who inducted him into covenant life. Alternatively, the nations themselves might decide, holding an auction or contest whenever new recruits join the covenant, perhaps having recruits be members of no nation until sufficient numbers of unplaced grogs are accrued. Finally, recruits could be born to a nation, either as the child of a grog, or a member of a specific ethnic group.

### 族群 (Nations)

另一种组织*龙套*的方式是按族群划分。一个由*龙套*组成的族群，可能包含仆役、专员与战士——甚至可能包括*同伴*与*法师*。族群的显著特征在于它横跨不同职能。一个人归属于哪个族群，可以通过多种方式决定：有时，新成员会被随机分配，或以抽签方式，或为补充某族群的人手空缺。也可以是：新成员加入将其引入*结社*生活之人（无论是*法师*还是其他人）所在的族群。又或者，由各族群自行竞争——每当有新成员加入*结社*时举行竞拍或比赛，可能规定新人暂时不隶属于任何族群，直到积累了足够数量的待分配*龙套*后再行分配。最后，成员也可以天生属于某个族群，无论是作为某名*龙套*的子嗣，还是作为某一特定族裔群体的成员。

Nation-level organizations are common in covenants where magi are relatively independent of each other. Each nation might be dedicated to the service of a single magus, or led by a companion instead. Members of nations often have a Loyalty Personality Trait specific to the nation as well as to the covenant.

族群层面的组织在*法师*彼此相对独立的*结社*中较为常见。每个族群可能专门服务于某位*法师*，或改由某位*同伴*领导。族群成员除了对*结社*整体的忠诚*性格特征*之外，往往还拥有一项专属于本族群的忠诚*性格特征*。

The most natural nations form around familial bonds. Old covenants, in particular, have covenfolk who were born in the covenant and have served the magi all their lives. These families can stretch back generations, each one tracing its ancestry back to one of the original servants of the covenant, or even one of the magi. Alternatively, a covenant might, at its foundation, have incorporated folk from the local area who were already strongly divided along family lines.

最自然形成的族群，往往以家族纽带为核心。尤其是古老的*结社*，其*结社民*往往生于*结社*、终生服务于*法师*。这些家族可以追溯数代，各家均将祖先追溯至*结社*最初的仆役之一，乃至某位*法师*。另一种情况是，*结社*在建立之初便纳入了当地已有强烈家族划分的居民。

The student bodies of some universities in Mythic Europe are divided into nations, with each nation responsible for the conduct and protection of each of its members (Art & Academe, page 94). These nations are usually loose geographical groups; the closest ethnic groups are well represented, whereas more distant students are lumped into much wider categories. Some covenants, particularly ones that straddle borders or have been formed from the merging of two or more turbs with distinct racial identities, use this structure.

*神话欧洲*部分大学的学生团体也按族群划分，各族群负责成员的行为与保护（*Art & Academe*，第 94 页）。这些族群通常是宽泛的地理群体：邻近族裔群体各自有代表，而较远地区的学生则被归入更宽泛的类别。某些*结社*——尤其是横跨边境、或由两支及以上具有不同种族认同的护卫队合并而成的*结社*——采用这一结构。

Nations may also form where a covenant is split over several geographically separated sites, or has deliberate spatial separation of its turb. There may be some separation of function in these nations; the nation that lives offshore probably has more sailors and fishermen than the nation that occupies the nearby hills, but both nations need their own specialists, soldiers, and servants.

当*结社*分布于多个地理上相互分隔的据点，或其护卫队刻意保持空间上的分离时，族群也可由此形成。这些族群之间可能存在一定的职能分工：例如，居于海边的族群或许拥有比占据附近山丘的族群更多的水手与渔夫，但两个族群都各自需要专员、战士与仆役。

### Guilds

An alternative to nations is guilds; that is, organizing the grogs by their function. Many covenants simply divide their covenants into servants, specialists, and soldiers, and consider these three "guilds" sufficient — and often they are. However, the larger covenants get, the more need there is to divide the grogs into smaller guilds with more defined functions. A covenant that has more than one source of income often institutes guilds as a means to organize collecting the revenue and conducting the necessary business that keeps the income sources operational.

### 行会 (Guilds)

行会是族群的另一种替代方案——即按职能组织*龙套*。许多*结社*只是将成员划分为仆役、专员与战士，认为这三个"行会"已经足够——而且往往确实如此。然而，*结社*规模越大，就越有必要将*龙套*细分为职能更明确的小型行会。拥有多个收入来源的*结社*，通常会设立行会，以此来组织收入征收和维持各收入来源正常运转所必需的业务。

There is also a tendency for grogs to selforganize. If there are a lot of craftsmen, then they tend to band together to mimic the guild structure of the outer world. If the covenant uses light cavalry as an elite unit, then these individuals may not want to fraternize with common foot soldiers.

*龙套*也有自发组织的倾向。若工匠众多，他们便会抱团，模仿外部世界的行会结构。若*结社*以轻骑兵作为精英单位，这些人或许不愿与普通步兵称兄道弟。

Note that the division of grogs into guilds does not necessarily mean literal guilds; that is, a political unit that can arrange simultaneous withdrawal of services in order to coerce better conditions for their masters.

需要注意的是，将*龙套*划分为行会，不一定意味着字面意义上的行会——即一种可以组织集体罢工、以此向主上施压争取更好待遇的政治单位。

#### Professional Guilds

Clearly a good model for covenant organization is the craft guilds, service guilds, and merchant guilds that are becoming more widespread in Mythic Europe in the 13th century. Once confined to big cities, guilds can now be found almost anywhere, at least in the West, and they regulate the quality of the goods produced by their members. A guild consists of all craftsmen that use the same or a group of closely allied Craft or Profession Abilities. However, at a covenant, a guild might exist for each Martial Ability, or perhaps even for select groups of Arcane or Supernatural Abilities. The guild provides incomes for the destitute and disabled, pays for the funeral services of deceased members, holds its own religious ceremonies, and sometimes even provides schooling for their members' children.

#### 职业行会 (Professional Guilds)

显然，在*神话欧洲*的十三世纪日益普及的手工业行会、服务行会与商人行会，是*结社*组织的绝佳模板。这些行会曾仅见于大城市，如今几乎遍及各地（至少在西方如此），负责规范成员的产品质量。一个行会由使用相同或一组密切相关工艺*技能*或职业*技能*的工匠组成。在*结社*中，行会也可以按每种战斗*技能*设立，甚至可以按奥法*技能*或超自然*技能*的特定分组设立。行会为贫困和残障成员提供收入，为已故成员支付丧葬费用，举行自己的宗教仪式，有时甚至为成员的子女提供教育。

At the head of a guild is the **dean**, elected by the guild members from among their number. The dean is supported by the aldermen (guild officials), who police the guild, ensure the quality of the work, and enforce the guild rules. Below the aldermen are the senior masters and masters. A master has sufficient skill to run his own shop, and has the right to attend meetings at the guildhall and have a say in the running of the guild. Senior masters have been masters for ten or more years, and may run more than one workshop, which can prove very lucrative. Each master might have one or two journeymen and several apprentices, who form the lowest rungs of the guild ladder. More information about guilds can be found in Chapter 3 of City & Guild.

行会由**会长**（dean）领导，从成员中选举产生。会长由执事（aldermen，即行会官员）辅佐，负责监督行会、确保工作质量并执行行会规则。执事之下是资深师傅与师傅。师傅拥有足够的技艺独立经营自己的作坊，并有权出席行会集会、参与行会事务的决策。资深师傅入行满十年或以上，可经营不止一间作坊，收益颇丰。每位师傅可能带有一两名熟练工（journeymen）和数名学徒，构成行会阶梯的最低几级。关于行会的更多信息，请参见 *City & Guild* 第三章。

The difference between a professional guild and other forms of governance is that the guild exists because its members want it. They have an expectation of advancement through the guild's ranks, and to one day have a say in how it is run. A guild might be a more familiar system for players who are used to concepts of social mobility and providing benefits for the hard workers, although this is an anachronism for much of Mythic Europe outside of the professional guilds.

职业行会与其他治理形式的区别在于：行会的存在是因为成员愿意如此。他们期待在行会中晋升，并期待有朝一日能参与行会事务的决策。对于习惯了社会流动性与多劳多得观念的玩家而言，行会制度或许更为熟悉——尽管这在*神话欧洲*大多数地方（职业行会以外）是一种时代错误。

#### Monastery

A monastery is designed along a guild structure. Unlike the clear hierarchy of the noble household, the abbot of a monastery designates officials to manage each aspect of the daily life of the monastery. These officials are then responsible for meeting the monastery's needs in their department, and can requisition manpower and finances as needed. This is a good model for a small covenant, where staff need not be permanently designated as a cook or a chambermaid, but instead take on such roles as requested.

#### 修道院 (Monastery)

修道院采用类似行会的结构设计。与贵族府邸那种层次分明的等级制不同，修道院的院长指定各官员分别管理修道院日常生活的各个方面。这些官员各负其责，须满足本部门的各项需求，并可根据需要调配人手与资金。这对小型*结社*而言是一个良好的模式——人员无需被永久指派为厨师或女仆，而是按需承担相应职责。

The abbot is the secular and spiritual leader of a monastery. He appoints various senior obedentiaries to care for different departments, so the terrar is in charge of the estate, the cellarer in charge of food, the bursar in charge of money, and so forth. Rather than having a staff or workforce assigned to each, these positions permit their holder to request the assistance of the other monks in the completion of their tasks. For more information on the internal structure of monasteries, see *The Church*, Chapter 4.

院长是修道院的世俗与精神领袖。他委任各位资深职事官员（obedentiaries）负责不同部门：地产官（terrar）管理庄园，膳食官（cellarer）负责伙食，司库（bursar）掌管财务，等等。这些职位不设专属人员，而是赋予持有者向其他修士请求协助以完成任务的权力。关于修道院内部结构的更多信息，请参见 *The Church* 第四章。

### No Formal Structure

Some covenants have never instituted any sort of structure at all among their covenfolk. This is usually only possible for very small numbers of covenfolk, without some sort of organization, important tasks are likely to be forgotten or neglected. In fact, if left to their own devices with no formal organization imposed upon them, covenfolk usually seek some sort of structure by themselves, it is natural for humans to seek social order.

### 无正式架构 (No Formal Structure)

有些*结社*从未在*结社民*之间建立任何形式的组织架构。这通常只在*结社民*人数极少时才可行——没有某种组织，重要任务就容易被遗忘或疏忽。事实上，若任由*结社民*自行其是而不加任何正式约束，他们往往会自发寻求某种秩序——追求社会秩序是人的天性。

If the covenfolk form a strong cohesive group, they can all work together toward the common good without needing a formal social structure. This is not to say that everyone does not know who is in charge, but there are fewer differences between servant, soldier, and specialist since everyone lends a hand in each task.

若*结社民*形成了一个凝聚力强的群体，他们可以无需正式社会架构，共同为公共利益而努力。这并非说没有人知道谁在主事，而是仆役、战士与专员之间的界限较少——因为每个人都会在各项任务中搭把手。

> ### Rivalries and Tension
>
> Any sort of social structure can lead to rivalries among the covenfolk. One nation may have deep-seated prejudices against another (perhaps arising from racial or cultural incompatibilities), or a guild might consider its tasks more important than those of the other guilds, and seek an improved status for its members.
> 
> In addition to tension between groups of covenfolk, there can also be tension between prominent individuals. If the covenfolk are split into families, there may be a longstanding feud between the leaders of two families; or there could be a power struggle within the turb over who deserves to be captain, once the present incumbent has retired. A common rivalry occurs between the steward and the chamberlain over precedence; many stewards believe that they should be the superior of the chamberlain. The turb captain need not be excluded from such rivalries; it is common for the captain to think himself more important than either, and he uses the armed men under his control to enforce this point.
> 
> The covenant of Tolosa Paratge, in Toulouse in the Provençal Tribunal, is completely split between two factions, the White and the Black, and this rivalry may bring the ancient covenant down completely (Faith & Flame, page 38).

> ### 竞争与矛盾 (Rivalries and Tension)
>
> 任何社会架构都可能在*结社民*之间引发竞争。一个族群可能对另一个族群抱有根深蒂固的偏见（也许源于种族或文化上的不相容），或者某个行会可能认为自己的职责比其他行会更重要，并寻求提升本行会成员的地位。
>
> 除群体之间的矛盾外，重要人物之间也可能存在张力。若*结社民*按家族划分，两个家族的领袖之间可能存在由来已久的宿怨；或者当现任护卫队长退休后，护卫队内部可能就谁更有资格继任而爆发权力争斗。管家与宫务官之间的地位之争也是常见的竞争——许多管家认为自己理应凌驾于宫务官之上。护卫队长也未必置身于这类竞争之外：队长通常认为自己比二者都重要，并以其掌控下的武装人员来强化这一立场。
>
> 普罗旺斯*仲裁*辖区图卢兹的 Tolosa Paratge *结社*，已完全分裂为白派与黑派两个派系，这场竞争可能将这个古老的*结社*彻底拖垮（*Faith & Flame*，第 38 页）。

## The Covenant in Play

Covenants do not gain Build Points while they are being played. Instead, the characters create enchanted devices, write books, and trade for further resources.

## 传奇中的结社 (The Covenant in Play)

*结社*在游戏过程中不会获得建造点数。取而代之，角色们通过制作*附魔装置*、撰写书籍以及交易来获取更多资源。

Events in play may lead to the covenant gaining or losing Boons or Hooks.

游戏中的事件可能导致*结社*获得或失去*契机*或*羁绊*。

If a Boon is lost, the characters should be given story opportunities to regain it. In effect, the lost Boon becomes a Hook.

若*契机*丧失，角色们应获得通过故事将其重新取回的机会。实际上，失去的*契机*变成了一个*羁绊*。

If a Boon is gained, the covenant should gain a corresponding Hook, representing the reaction of the rest of the world to the change in its fortunes.

若*契机*获得，*结社*应随之获得一个相对应的*羁绊*，代表世界其余部分对*结社*命运转变的反应。

If a Hook is lost, because the situation is resolved, that is fine, and part of the natural growth of the saga.

若*羁绊*因情势得到解决而消失，这完全正常，是*传奇*自然发展的一部分。

If a Hook is gained, again, that is fine.

若*羁绊*增加，同样没有问题。

Not all Hooks can be removed, but if a particular Hook can be overcome it should take at least half a dozen stories for a minor Hook, and around twenty for a major Hook. The Hooks are supposed to drive a significant portion of the saga, so they should not be resolved until they have done so.

并非所有*羁绊*都可以消除，但若某个*羁绊*确实可以被克服，则次要*羁绊*至少需要约六个故事，主要*羁绊*则约需二十个故事。*羁绊*本应推动*传奇*的重要部分，因此在它充分发挥这一作用之前，不应被轻易解决。
